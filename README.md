


# Project Background

The labor market is evolving rapidly due to technological advancements, demographic shifts, and changing industry demands. Organizations, HR professionals, workforce planners, and policymakers require reliable labor market intelligence to understand which occupations are expected to grow, the skills employers value most, and how workforce demand is expected to change over time. Making informed decisions about hiring, workforce development, and talent investment requires access to accurate, data-driven insights.

This project leverages publicly available data from the **U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Employment Projections (2024–2034)** and the **O*NET Database** to build an interactive Labor Market Analytics Dashboard. The dashboard integrates employment projections, annual wages, education requirements, job preparation levels, software skills, in-demand skills, hot technologies, knowledge areas, and career pathways into a unified business intelligence solution.

By transforming raw labor market data into meaningful visualizations and executive-level insights, the project enables stakeholders to identify high-growth occupations, evaluate hiring demand, benchmark compensation, understand emerging skill requirements, and support strategic workforce planning. The dashboard serves as a decision-support tool for businesses, HR teams, educational institutions, and government agencies seeking to align talent strategies with future labor market trends between **2024 and 2034**.

# Key Metrics

* **Employment Growth Rate (%)** – Measures the projected percentage growth in employment between **2024 and 2034**, helping identify occupations expected to experience the fastest workforce expansion.

* **Average Annual Job Openings** – Represents the average number of job openings projected each year, providing insight into occupations with the highest hiring demand and recruitment opportunities.

* **Average Annual Wage (2024)** – Displays the average annual earnings across occupations, enabling compensation benchmarking and analysis of high- and low-paying career paths.

* **Occupations Requiring In-Demand Skills** – Counts the number of occupations requiring skills currently identified as in demand, highlighting workforce readiness and the importance of upskilling for future employment.

## Average Annual Job Openings Analysis (2024–2034)

| **Section** | **Details** |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Overview** | This visualization highlights the **Top 10 occupations** with the highest average annual job openings projected between **2024 and 2034**, based on the **U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Employment Projections**. Unlike employment growth rate, average annual job openings represent total hiring demand generated from **new job creation, employee retirements, career transitions, and workforce replacement**. This analysis helps organizations identify occupations expected to experience the greatest recruitment activity over the next decade. |
| **Key Takeaways** | • **Baristas** and **Fast Food and Counter Workers** generate the highest projected hiring demand, each averaging approximately **904 thousand** annual job openings, reflecting significant workforce turnover and sustained consumer demand.<br><br>• **Retail Salespersons (556 thousand)** and **Cashiers (543 thousand)** continue to require substantial annual hiring despite technological advancements.<br><br>• **Stockers and Order Fillers**, **Waiters and Waitresses**, **Laborers and Freight, Stock, and Material Movers**, and **Customer Service Representatives** remain among the occupations with the largest annual recruitment needs. |
| **Business Insights** | • High annual job openings are primarily driven by **workforce turnover and replacement demand**, rather than exceptionally high employment growth rates.<br><br>• The **retail, hospitality, food service, logistics, and customer service** industries will continue to require significant workforce recruitment throughout the **2024–2034** projection period due to their large employment bases.<br><br>• Average annual job openings support **recruitment planning, workforce forecasting, and talent acquisition**, enabling organizations to anticipate long-term hiring needs. |
| **Recommendations** | • Strengthen recruitment pipelines and employee retention strategies for **food service, retail, hospitality, logistics, and customer service** occupations.<br><br>• Prioritize workforce development and training initiatives to prepare job seekers for occupations with consistently high hiring demand.<br><br>• Combine **employment growth projections** with **average annual job openings** when making workforce planning decisions to build effective long-term recruitment strategies. |

## Job Zone Analysis: Occupation Distribution and Annual Wage (2024)

| **Section** | **Details** |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Overview** | This visualization compares the **number of occupations** within each **Job Zone** category against the **total annual wages (2024)**. The analysis demonstrates how education, experience, and preparation requirements influence both the availability of occupations and overall earning potential. It provides valuable insights for workforce planners, educators, employers, and job seekers seeking to understand the relationship between job accessibility and compensation. |
| **Key Takeaways** | • **Basic Preparation Required** contains the largest number of occupations (**329**) and contributes approximately **$16.0M** in total annual wages, indicating that entry-level occupations account for a significant portion of the labor market.<br><br>• **Extensive Preparation and Advanced Expertise Required** has the fewest occupations (**171**) but generates the highest total annual wages (**$20.1M**), reflecting the premium associated with highly specialized education, experience, and expertise.<br><br>• As job preparation requirements increase from **Basic** to **Advanced**, the number of occupations gradually decreases while total wage potential increases, highlighting a positive relationship between specialization and compensation. |
| **Business Insights** | • **Entry-level occupations** offer the greatest employment availability, making them essential for supporting large-scale workforce participation and addressing high-volume hiring needs.<br><br>• **Advanced Job Zone** occupations represent smaller talent pools but deliver significantly higher economic value, emphasizing the importance of investing in specialized skills, professional education, and workforce development.<br><br>• Organizations should maintain a balanced talent strategy by recruiting for high-volume operational roles while simultaneously developing succession plans for specialized, high-value positions requiring advanced expertise. |
| **Recommendations** | • Invest in **continuous learning, certifications, and advanced education** programs to help employees transition into higher Job Zone occupations with greater earning potential.<br><br>• Strengthen workforce planning by balancing recruitment efforts across both **entry-level** and **specialized** occupations to ensure long-term organizational resilience.<br><br>• Collaborate with educational institutions and training providers to develop talent pipelines for high-preparation occupations expected to drive future business growth and innovation. |
